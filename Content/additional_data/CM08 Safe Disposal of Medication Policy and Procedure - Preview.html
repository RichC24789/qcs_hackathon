3. Objectives

3.1

{{{Client_Name_Official}}} promotes safe practice and the efficient management of medications and is committed to the minimisation and avoidance of unnecessary medicines waste.

4. Policy

4.1

{{{Client_Name_Official}}} understands that medicines that have been prescribed for and dispensed to individual {{{Service_user_text}}}s remain their property. Consent will be sought before medicines are disposed of and {{{Client_Name_Official}}} will ensure that {{{SupportWorkerOrCarer}}}s are aware and follow the procedures for the safe disposal of medication which includes requirements for record keeping and safe storage.
4.2

The disposal of medicines is regulated by The Controlled Waste (England and Wales) Regulations 2012. Under these regulations, medicines fall under the category of clinical waste.

In non-nursing homes, clinical waste is treated as household waste, and medicines should be returned to the pharmacy that supplied it for disposal.

{{{Reg_Manager_Name}}} is responsible for ensuring that this is determined within the Service Level Agreement with the pharmacy. Pharmacy details: {{{pharmacy_address}}}
4.3

Disposal Applies To:

  • Medication remaining after the {{{Service_user_text}}} has died
  • Medication remaining after the {{{Service_user_text}}} has left {{{Client_Name_Official}}}
  • Medication that has been discontinued
  • Dispensed refused doses
  • Medication that is past its expiry date
4.4

Storing unwanted medicines increases risk to {{{Service_user_text}}}s at {{{Client_Name_Official}}}.

  • Medicines belonging to {{{Service_user_text}}}s who are no longer at {{{Client_Name_Official}}} could be given to another {{{Service_user_text}}} in error
  • Discontinued medication could still be given
  • Expired medication could still be given
4.5

{{{Reg_Manager_Name}}} will ensure that all unwanted medicines are disposed of promptly.
4.6

Disposal of Controlled Drugs (CDs) is detailed in the Controlled Drugs Policy and Procedure.

5. Procedure

5.1

General Disposal Procedures

  • Unwanted medicines (including partially used containers) must be returned to the pharmacy that supplies {{{Client_Name_Official}}} if they have appropriate arrangements in place and agree to disposal
  • Medicines no longer required by the {{{Service_user_text}}} should be disposed of with their consent
  • All unused medications will be disposed of in the disposal container supplied by the pharmacy and covered by a written record of the returns
  • The disposal container must be kept securely locked away until it is collected by the pharmacy
  • {{{Service_user_text}}}s' prescribed medication that is no longer required must never be used for any other {{{Service_user_text}}}
  • Refused medication should not be returned to the packaging, but must be disposed of in the correct manner. Medication that is refused after it has been in the {{{Service_user_text}}}'s mouth should be safely put into a yellow clinical waste bag
  • Medicines for disposal must be stored securely and separately to medicines that are in use
  • Do not dispose of medicines through the sewage system at {{{Client_Name_Official}}}
  • Do not remove tablets or capsules from blister strips
  • Medication should be removed from the Monitored Dosage Systems (MDS)
  • Disposal will be accurately recorded 

Empty containers, used medicated dressings and patches should be disposed of as follows:

  • Used medicated dressings patches must be folded in half and placed in a yellow clinical waste bag
  • Emptied cream tubs/tubes must be disposed of in a yellow clinical waste bag
  • Empty inhalers or medicated aerosols should be returned to the community pharmacy for proper disposal
  • Other empty containers/bottles must be thoroughly rinsed out and placed in domestic waste
  • Equipment used to administer medication, e.g. droppers, plastic spoons and measuring pots to be placed in a yellow clinical waste bag
  • Empty cardboard boxes can be thrown away in general waste

Any queries relating to the correct way to dispose of medication should be discussed with the advisory pharmacist.
5.2

Records

A record of the medication for disposal (disposal record book/file) must be kept by {{{Client_Name_Official}}}, and must include:

  • Date of disposal or return to pharmacy
  • Name of the {{{Service_user_text}}}
  • Name, form and strength of medicine
  • Quantity of medication to be disposed of
  • Reason for disposal
  • Signature of staff member arranging disposal
    • Signature of witness if available and required by {{{Client_Name_Official}}}
  • Signature of the person collecting the medicines for disposal
  • A duplicate copy must be retained by {{{Client_Name_Official}}}

Records should also be made on the {{{Service_user_text}}}'s {{{MAR_Chart}}}.

A 'Record of Medications Returned to Pharmacy' document can be found in the Forms section of this policy.
5.3

Some medicines are classed as hazardous waste and {{{SupportWorkerOrCarer}}}s must ensure that waste medicines are segregated correctly. Advice must be obtained from the licensed waste carrier, then documented and followed. Staff should refer to the Healthcare Waste Policy and Procedure.
5.4

Unused medicines dispensed in a monitored dosage system must be discarded no later than 8 weeks after the dispensing date.

Likewise, many creams, ointments or liquids may have specific expiry dates.

Staff must familiarise themselves with these in accordance with manufacturers' guidelines or on the advice of {{{pharmacy_address}}}.
5.5

Medication belonging to recently deceased {{{Service_user_text}}}s must be kept locked away separately from other medicines for seven days before being returned to the pharmacy for disposal. This is in case the coroner's office, police or courts require them as evidence as part of any investigation into the death of the {{{Service_user_text}}}.
5.6

Confidentiality

All pharmacy labels will be removed before containers/tubes are disposed of and labels shredded or the confidential text overwritten with a black marker. Any removed labels should be shredded.
5.7

Governance

{{{Reg_Manager_Name}}} is responsible for ensuring that the safe disposal of medicines at {{{Client_Name_Official}}} is regularly audited as part of the medication audit process.

Staff should refer to the Auditing and Monitoring of Medication Policy and Procedure at {{{Client_Name_Official}}}.

6. Definitions

6.1 Hazardous Waste
  • Waste is considered 'hazardous' under environmental legislation when it contains substances or has properties that might make it harmful to human health or the environment
6.2 Monitored Dosing System (MDS)
  • Monitored dosage systems are also known as blister packing. This is where medicines are placed in separate compartments or blister packs according to the time needed to take them. The time is clearly labelled on the packs and this ensures that the correct medication is taken at the correct time. This system allows the keeping of medicines in order and prevents any confusion about what tablets must be taken at a particular time

7. Key Facts - Professionals

  • Medicines for disposal must be stored separately from medicines in use
  • Medicines for disposal must be stored securely until they are collected
  • All staff with responsibility for medication administration must be aware of, and knowledgeable about, the method of returning unused/date expired drugs
  • Medication belongs to the {{{Service_user_text}}} and consent must be obtained before it is returned to the pharmacy
  • Medication belonging to another {{{Service_user_text}}} must never be used for someone else
  • {{{Client_Name_Official}}} must keep records of medicines (including controlled drugs) that have been returned to the pharmacy

8. Key Facts - People Affected by The Service

  • If we support you with medication, we will ensure that we gain your consent before returning it to the pharmacist if it is no longer needed
  • Your medication will not be given to anyone else and belongs to you
  • If you have any queries relating to your medication, please speak to a member of staff

Outstanding Practice

To be "outstanding" in this policy area you could provide evidence that:
  • Records surrounding medication return or refusal are extremely clear, well ordered and provide an efficient audit trail
  • There is evidence that {{{Service_user_text}}}s are supported to self-manage their medication where they have been assessed as having the capacity to do so. Staff understand the Mental Capacity Act and can apply it in practice
  • {{{Client_Name_Official}}} is able to demonstrate that excess medicine is not wasted through careful ordering of medicines. This can be evidenced through medicine waste records
  • There are clear records and evidence of medicine waste returned to the pharmacy
