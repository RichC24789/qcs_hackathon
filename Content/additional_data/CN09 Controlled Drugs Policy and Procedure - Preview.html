3. Objectives

3.1

To ensure that the supply, receipt, storage, administration and disposal of controlled drugs (CDs) meets all regulatory requirements.
3.2

All staff involved in the administration of controlled drugs follow the '6 Rights of Medication Administration'.
3.3

To ensure that there are procedures in place for identifying, reporting and reviewing incidents, errors and near misses involving CDs as well as sharing concerns about the mishandling of CDs.

4. Policy

4.1

The Misuse of Drugs Act 1971 places controls on certain medicines. These are referred to as controlled drugs (CDs).

The Misuse of Drugs Regulations 2001 splits controlled drugs into 5 schedules, which specify the requirements that apply to them.

  • Schedule 1 - includes drugs not used medicinally
  • Schedule 2 - are subject to storage and recording conditions (e.g. morphine, diamorphine, methadone, oxycodone, fentanyl)
  • Schedule 3 - some are subject to storage conditions (e.g. gabapentin, midazolam, pregabalin, temazepam, tramadol)
  • Schedule 4 and 5 - no conditions (e.g. schedule 4 - zopiclone, diazepam, schedule 5 - codeine)

A list of commonly used controlled drugs can be found at GOV.UK guidance 'List of most commonly encountered drugs currently controlled under the misuse of drugs legislation' (a link can be found in the further reading section).
4.2

{{{Client_Name_Official}}} will ensure that all medication trained staff are aware of, and follow, the policy and associated procedures relating to the safe and secure handling and storage of controlled drugs in accordance with legal, regulatory and good practice guidance specified by the Department of Health, the {{{reg_authority_name_long}}} and the Royal Pharmaceutical Society.

{{{Client_Name_Official}}} will ensure, through the use of this policy and procedure, that:

  • Staff are clear on the standards that are expected of them in relation to the handling and storage of controlled drugs
  • Staff and visitors are not put at risk as a result of the incorrect handling of controlled drug medicines
  • All legislation and guidance are adhered to with respect to controlled drugs
  • Risks associated with the incorrect handling and storage of controlled drugs are reduced to a minimum
  • There are robust systems for ordering, storing, transporting, administering, recording and disposing of CDs safely
4.3

Controlled Drug Lead 

Designated bodies are required under the Controlled Drug 2013 regulations to have a Controlled Drugs Accountable Officer (CDAO). 

These organisations include:

  • NHS trusts
  • Independent hospitals in England
  • NHS England Local Area Teams
  • Headquarters in England of the regular or reserved Armed Forces

However, a wide range of organisations still prescribe and/or handle controlled drugs in their day-to-day work, but do not meet the definition of a designated body.

These organisations should ensure they have a nominated person as a lead for controlled drugs to enable relevant focus on governance and oversight, ultimately helping to ensure safe care for {{{Service_user_text}}} (CQC 2025).

{{{Reg_Manager_Name}}} will nominate a lead for controlled drugs at {{{Client_Name_Official}}}.

5. Procedure

5.1

Self-Administration

{{{Service_user_text}}}s who wish to self-administer controlled drugs must have a risk assessment in place to consider their safe storage.

Staff should refer to the Overarching Medicines Management Policy and Procedure at {{{Client_Name_Official}}} for guidance on self-administration.

Sensible precautions are important to make sure that controlled drugs are kept safely when with the {{{Service_user_text}}}. {{{Client_Name_Official}}} does not need a CD cupboard in each bedroom. However, a lockable cupboard or drawer is essential.

The risk assessment process places responsibility on the {{{Service_user_text}}} who keeps the controlled drug. Through monitoring and review of the risk factors, {{{Client_Name_Official}}} should identify that controlled drugs are not left lying around where they could be taken by someone else. There is no need to keep a record in the CD Register when the {{{Service_user_text}}} is wholly independent; that is, they are responsible for requesting a prescription and collecting the controlled drugs personally from the pharmacy.

If the {{{Service_user_text}}} does not arrange the supply and collection of controlled drugs but relies on {{{Client_Name_Official}}} to do so, there should be clear records in the CD Register, including:

  • Receipt from the pharmacy
  • Supply to the {{{Service_user_text}}}
  • Any subsequent disposal of unwanted controlled drugs
5.2

Ordering and Receipt

The quantity of controlled drugs supplied should not exceed 30 days.

{{{Reg_Manager_Name}}} must ensure there is a clear process for the safe handling of controlled drugs when they are being delivered to {{{Client_Name_Official}}}.

CDs should be delivered separately from the main delivery of medicines and the package must be clearly marked that it contains a CD.

Medication trained staff should sign for the CDs from the delivery driver and must ensure:

  • CDs are in a separate package on delivery and clearly marked
  • CDs are checked, signed for and witnessed by two staff members who are trained and competent
  • CDs are entered into the CD Register immediately
  • They are not left out on the side or in the cupboard to be entered later
  • CDs received are correct against copies of the original request or prescription. This check includes:
    • Drug name
    • Quantity - Staff should physically count tablets, capsules, ampoules, prefilled syringes and patches ensuring that the quantity received matches the quantity on the label and on the Medicines Administration Chart (MAR). For liquids, check that the seal has not been tampered with
    • Formulation – Tablets, capsules, patches, injections, liquids, etc.
    • Strength – milligram per unit dose or volume
    • Expiry date of preparation 
    • Preparation fit for use i.e., no damage 
    • Clear dose instructions
  • Discrepancies are dealt with as per policy
  • After the checks have been performed and are correct, the MAR can be signed identifying the quantity received and dated by the receiving member of staff

When staff sign the CDs in from the delivery driver, they are taking responsibility for those medicines.

If a discrepancy is identified between what is expected and the supply received, then staff should:

  • Report immediately to {{{Reg_Manager_Name}}}  
  • Record the stock received into the CD Register
  • Store the incorrect CD stock received separately in the CD cabinet until the discrepancy can be resolved
  • Contact the supplier of the CD to report the discrepancy
  • Arrange for the supplier to collect the incorrect CD
  • When the incorrect CD supply is collected by the supplier:
    • Obtain a signed receipt
    • Make an entry in the CD Register to record this
5.3

Stock of Controlled Drugs

All CDs in {{{Client_Name_Official}}} must be prescribed for individual {{{Service_user_text}}}s by an appropriately qualified healthcare professional and dispensed by a pharmacy or dispensing practice.

Care homes that provide nursing care may hold stocks of CDs in schedules 3, 4 and 5 without the need to have a Home Office licence. For example, for {{{Service_user_text}}}s nearing the end of their lives.

Stock of controlled drugs in schedule 2 can be held in care homes with nursing where the home is wholly or mainly maintained (50% or more) by a public authority, out of public funds, by charity, or voluntary subscriptions (Home Office 2014). Otherwise care homes with nursing must apply for a UK Home Office Controlled Drugs licence (RCN 2024).
5.4

Storage

{{{Client_Name_Official}}} is required to comply with the CD safe custody arrangements.

Schedule 2:

  • Must be stored in a controlled drugs cupboard
  • Must keep records in a Controlled Drug Register

Schedule 3:

  • Do not need to be recorded in the Controlled Drug Register
    • Some care homes may choose to do so, which is good practice
  • Some need to be stored in the controlled drug cupboard
    • Temazepam, buprenorphine
  • Some do not need to be stored in the controlled drug cupboard
    • Pregabalin, gabapentin, tramadol, midazolam

Local policies will need to be adhered to in relation to the storage and recording of Schedule 3 CDs exempt from secure storage and recording under the legislation.

If staff are unsure what the legal requirements are, then they should contact the pharmacy.

Schedule 4 and 5:

  • Do not need to be recorded in the Controlled Drug Register
  • Do not need to be stored in the controlled drug cupboard
  • Some services may choose to do so:
    • Oramorph® oral solution 10mg/5ml is not a schedule 2 CD
    • It is good practice to store it in a CD cabinet and complete CD records

The CD Cupboard:

  • Must comply with the requirements laid out in the Misuse of Drugs (Safe Custody) Regulations, including:
    • Locked non-portable cabinet 
    • Metal construction of specified gauge 
    • Being fixed securely to a solid wall or floor with the specified bolts
    • Fitted with a robust lock and strong hinges
  • Suppliers of CD cabinets can confirm that a cupboard meets the legal requirements. {{{Client_Name_Official}}} will request formal confirmation when purchasing a CD cabinet
  • A label should be fitted to the inside of the door displaying manufacturer name and other specific information
  • It is a commonly held belief that a CD cupboard must be a ‘cupboard within a cupboard’ but this is not the case
  • Nothing should be displayed outside a safe or cabinet to indicate that drugs are kept inside it
  • For safe practice, the controlled drug cupboards should only be used for the storage of controlled drugs
    • Items such as jewellery or money should not be placed in the cabinet
  • If medication is provided in a monitored dosage system (MDS), the MDS should be stored in the CD cabinet

Key Management:

  • Only those with authorised access should hold keys to the controlled drug cupboard and a log should be kept of keyholders
  • The Nurse in charge for each shift is responsible for the keys to the CD cupboard
  • The keys must be kept on the person of the Nurse in charge
  • Key-holding may be delegated to other suitably-trained Nurses or medication trained staff, but the responsibility rests with the Nurse in charge
  • At the end of each shift the Nurse in Charge for the shift is responsible for handing the controlled drug keys over to the incoming Nurse in charge
  • Staff requiring access to the controlled drug cupboard must return the keys to the nurse in charge when they have finished with the keys
  • {{{Client_Name_Official}}} should consider maintaining a register for the keys with time, date and signature
  • Spare keys should be stored securely
  • If the CD keys cannot be found, every effort must be made to find them as soon as possible . If there are doubts about the security of controlled drugs, {{{Reg_Manager_Name}}} should risk assess if the locks need to be changed
    • Spare key must only be issued in an emergency in order to access the CD cupboard for {{{Service_user_text}}} care
    • If the location of the missing key is not established by the end of the appropriate shift then the locks must be changed

Refrigerating Controlled Drugs:

If a CD needs to be stored in the fridge, then it should be stored in a locked medicines fridge. The CD should be stored separately to other medications in a lockable box within the fridge.

Medicinal and Food Grade Cannabis Products

Cannabis based products for medicinal use (CBPMs) are Schedule 2 controlled drugs.

Over the counter food grade cannabis products as food supplements are not medicines and if purchased for their own (or a relative's) use, a risk assessment must be in place.
5.5

Recording of Named {{{Service_user_text}}}s' Controlled Drugs

For medicines that are controlled drugs, and subject to CD recording requirements, {{{Client_Name_Official}}} must keep a separate CD Register in addition to the record on the {{{MAR_Chart}}}

The CD Register:

  • The CD register must be a bound book with numbered pages
  • There must be a separate page for each form and strength of each controlled drug for each {{{Service_user_text}}}
  • The name, form and strength of the drug is specified at the top of each page
  • An index page should be maintained in the CD register, indicating for individual {{{Service_user_text}}}s on which page of the CD register each CD can be found
  • All entries must be in ink or made using an indelible ballpoint pen
  • Entries must be in chronological order
  • The member of staff making the entry must sign and date it
  • Whenever possible, all entries should also be witnessed by a suitably trained member of staff who should also sign the entry
  • A running balance must be kept and checked for every entry
  • An electronic register which complies with the current guidance may be kept. {{{Client_Name_Official}}} will seek advice before using an electronic CD Register
  • The CD Register must be used to record the receipt, administration, transfer or disposal of CDs
  • The entry must be made as soon as possible
  • The CD Register should be kept for two years from the last entry
  • Good practice would be to keep the CD Register for longer, as cases can take several years to come to light or before they go to court
  • When transferring a CD to a new page in the CD Register:
    • State the page it is carried forward to at the bottom of the page
    • State the page it has been carried over from at the top of the new page, with ‘carried forward from page…’
    • Update the index at the front of the register including page numbers when CDs are carried forward
  • Errors made in the CD Register must never be altered or crossed out
  • If an error is made in the CD Register, the incorrect entry should be put in brackets with a note made:
    • At the bottom of the page with the phrase ‘entered in error’, or
    • ‘Entered in error’ can be written on a new line below the incorrect entry
    • The incorrect entry should be signed, witnessed, and dated
    • The correct entry should be made using a new line
  • The register should not be used for any other purpose
  • The register must be kept in a secure place when not in use

Electronic registers are permitted as an alternative to a bound book, legislation requires that computerised entries must:

  • Be attributable to the person who created the record
  • Be secure
  • Not be cancelled or altered
  • Be able to be corrected and dated when the correction is made
  • Be able to be audited
  • Comply with best practice
  • Be accessible from {{{Client_Name_Official}}} and capable of being printed
5.6

The Following Details Should be Recorded on the Correct Page in the Register:

All entries in the Controlled Drug Register should be made by 2 staff members:

  • The registered nurse and
  • Witnessed by a second medication trained and competent staff member. Where possible, this should be a second registered nurse
    • If not, a medication trained and competent {{{SupportWorkerOrCarer}}} can witness

Receipt: On the day of receipt, record the date of receipt, where the medication was received from and the quantity received. A running balance should be kept and updated.

Disposal: Record the date of disposal, the quantity disposed of, how the medication was disposed of and the reason for disposal. Update the running balance.

Transfers: Record the date of transfer, the quantity transferred and to whom (or where) the medication was transferred. Update the running balance.

Routine checks: The balance of all controlled drugs recorded in the CD Register/Record Book must be checked and reconciled with the stock amounts in the cupboard, and recorded. The frequency of such checks is determined by risk assessment and ensures that discrepancies are identified promptly. Balance checks should be carried out at least weekly.
5.7

Dealing with Discrepancies

{{{Client_Name_Official}}} must ensure that any discrepancies are investigated without delay and a local incident form completed.

If a discrepancy is identified between calculated stock figures (running balances) and actual stock, staff should:

  • Inform {{{Reg_Manager_Name}}} who should investigate immediately
  • Check back through the entries in the CD Register to ensure that there has not been an administrative error such as a missed or incorrect entry or a calculation error
  • Check the {{{MAR_Chart}}} and records of disposed medicines

If the discrepancy can be identified, record the outcome:

  • DO NOT cancel, cross out or through, obliterate and/or alter any entry in the CD Register
  • Under the last entry, details of the following should be made:
    • The date
    • The error in subtraction/addition (indicated with an asterisk)
    • The correct balance
    • The signature of the registered nurse and the witnessing staff member
    • Reference to any supporting documentation that was used to resolve the discrepancy

Unresolved discrepancies must be reported; this should include the regional NHS Controlled Drugs Accountable Officer (CDAO) at NHS England or the police, depending on the circumstances, and the {{{reg_authority}}} if the incident meets the criteria of a statutory notification.
5.8

Administration of Controlled Drugs

In addition to the procedures relating to the administration and recording of other medicines outlined in the Administration of Medicines Policy and Procedure, the following procedures must be carried out when administering controlled drugs:

  • The administration of a controlled drug should be by a registered nurse
  • It must be witnessed by a second staff member and where possible, this should be a second registered nurse
  • If not, a medication trained and competent {{{SupportWorkerOrCarer}}} can witness
  • They must witness the whole procedure
  • The stock balance should be confirmed prior to proceeding with administration
  • If incorrect, the {{{Registered_Manager_JobTitle}}} must be informed
  • See above for discrepancies
  • There should not be unnecessary delay in the {{{Service_user_text}}} receiving their medication
  • The stock balance, if different, should be clearly documented in the register before proceeding with administration
  • Record the time and date of administration and the dose to be administered
  • After administration, the Register must be signed by the registered nurse administering the medication and the witness, and the running balance updated
  • The {{{Service_user_text}}}’s {{{MAR_Chart}}} must be signed by the member of staff who administered the medication. It is good practice for this to be countersigned by the staff member who witnessed the medication administration
  • Where part of an injectable CD is administered, the CD Register/Record Book details the amount given and the amount denatured/disposed of
  • When administering topical controlled drugs, records should include the site of application and the frequency of rotation of the site
5.9

Disposal of Controlled Drugs

Disposal of Named {{{Service_user_text}}}s' CDs:

  • When controlled drugs have passed their expiry date, the need for prescription has ceased, or the {{{Service_user_text}}} has died, the controlled drugs should be denatured using a recognised CD denaturing kit before consigning to a licensed waste company. (Some pharmacies are also willing to take back medication from nursing homes, but the home must ensure that the pharmacy has appropriate arrangements in place)
    • Advice on using denaturing kits can be found in the Further Reading Section
  • Even when still in date, such drugs should not be reused for other {{{Service_user_text}}}s
  • {{{Client_Name_Official}}} must have a T28 exemption from the Environment Agency in place for the denaturing of CDs on site, a link can be found in the Further Reading section of this policy
  • {{{Client_Name_Official}}} must keep a copy of the Waste Transfer Note and make a record in its usual record of 'disposed of' medication
  • Denaturing should be performed by the registered nurse and witnessed
  • {{{Reg_Manager_Name}}} must ensure that unwanted CDs are denatured promptly and correctly, and collected as required
  • Relevant details of any such disposal should be entered into the ‘CD Register’ and signed by the authorised member of staff denaturing the drug and the witness
  • Appropriate records should also show:
    • The date
    • The controlled drug name and strength
    • The number or volume of tablets, liquid or patches
    • The signature of the authorised staff member
    • The signature of the witness
  • Individual doses of CDs which have been prepared but not administered are denatured and witnessed (in accordance with local risk assessment/procedures), and the reason documented

Disposal of CD Patches:

  • Used controlled drug patches, such as Fentanyl, still contain controlled drugs even after use and therefore should be folded in half and denatured as above
  • Unused CD patches should also be denatured as above

Stock Controlled Drugs:

  • Staff must denature out-of-date stocks of controlled drugs in Schedules 2, 3 and 4 (Part I) before handing to the waste disposal company
  • Staff must destroy Schedule 2 stock in the presence of an authorised witness. This includes a police constable or inspectors of the General Pharmaceutical Council. The lead NHS England controlled drugs accountable officer may also appoint authorised witnesses (CQC 2025)
  • Staff need to record details of the destruction in the controlled drugs register. It is good practice for another member of staff to witness the denaturing of stock in Schedules 3 and 4 (Part I)
5.10

Booking Out Named {{{Service_user_text}}} Controlled Drugs

Appropriate entries MUST BE RECORDED in the Controlled Drugs Register at {{{Client_Name_Official}}}. Records should indicate the date of discharge of the {{{Service_user_text}}} and the details of the medicines the {{{Service_user_text}}} has taken out of {{{Client_Name_Official}}}.

  • There should always be two medication trained staff booking out the medicines
  • The controlled drugs for booking out must be checked and counted against the {{{Service_user_text}}}'s {{{MAR_Chart}}} and checked for quantity left against instructions on the label on the medicine's original container
  • The balance of controlled drugs being booked out must be recorded in the Controlled Drugs Register and signed and dated by each authorised person
  • {{{Service_user_text}}}s or their representative should sign to say that they have taken the medicines out of {{{Client_Name_Official}}}
  • Medicines for self-administering {{{Service_user_text}}}s do not have to be booked out; this includes controlled drugs
  • Any discrepancies must be brought to the notice of the {{{Registered_Manager_JobTitle}}}
5.11



End Of Life Care

There should be appropriate access to controlled drugs at {{{Client_Name_Official}}} for {{{Service_user_text}}}s who require them, especially at the end of their lives.


5.12

Statutory Notifications

There is no requirement to notify CQC about medicines errors, but you must notify them if a medicines error has caused:

  • A death
  • An injury
  • Abuse, or an allegation of abuse
  • An incident reported to or investigated by the police

This includes where any of these have been caused by a controlled drug.

Staff can download the tool from the CQC website - a link can be found in the Further Reading section of this policy. The tool is used anonymously and CQC do not have access to user details.

6. Definitions

6.1 Denaturing
  • Out of date or unwanted controlled drugs must be denatured prior to disposal so they cannot be retrieved, recovered or reused
  • Controlled drug denaturing kits will render unwanted medicines irretrievable and unfit for use, ready to be disposed of

7. Key Facts - Professionals

  • CDs prescribed by medical practitioners must comply with specific legal requirements
  • Staff must ensure that any administration, receipt or return to pharmacy of controlled drugs is logged in the Controlled Drug Register at {{{Client_Name_Official}}}
  • Providers must have a CD cabinet that complies with the requirements laid out in the Misuse of Drugs (Safe Custody) Regulations and this includes being fixed securely to a solid wall or floor with specified fixings
  • CD audits that check stock and expiry dates are necessary and must be evidenced within the Controlled Drug Book
  • All controlled drugs, when no longer required, will be denatured and disposed of by a licensed waste disposal company

8. Key Facts - People Affected by The Service

  • {{{Service_user_text}}}s may self-administer controlled drugs
