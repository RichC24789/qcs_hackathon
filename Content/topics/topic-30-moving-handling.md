# Topic 30: Safe moving and handling — technique, risk assessment, legislation

**Theme:** Moving & handling
**Primary format:** Animation
**Secondary format:** Quick reference card

---

## Hook

Back injuries are among the most common workplace injuries in care — and most of them are preventable with the right technique and equipment.

---

## Key points

- Never lift a person manually — use appropriate equipment and techniques
- Every service user must have a moving and handling risk assessment in their care plan
- Before any moving and handling task, check the care plan, prepare the environment, and communicate with the service user
- Two staff are required for many moving and handling tasks — do not attempt alone if the care plan specifies two
- Report any equipment faults or concerns about a service user's mobility immediately

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Service user has had a change in mobility and the care plan has not been updated?** → Do not proceed with the existing plan — report to your manager and request a reassessment
- **Equipment is unavailable or faulty?** → Do not improvise — report to your manager and do not proceed until appropriate equipment is available
- **Service user refuses to use equipment?** → Record the refusal, inform your manager, and explore whether alternative equipment or approach might be acceptable

---

## Common mistakes

- Attempting a two-person task alone because a colleague is unavailable
- Not checking the moving and handling care plan before starting
- Bending at the waist rather than the knees when positioning equipment

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — for any change in a service user's mobility, equipment faults, or concerns about a task
- **Occupational therapist** — for reassessments following a change in mobility

---

## Animation notes

Demonstrate safe transfer technique from bed to chair using a hoist — correct positioning, communication with the service user, and two-person process. Use illustrated characters rather than live video. 90 seconds.
