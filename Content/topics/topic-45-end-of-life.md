# Topic 45: End of life care — documentation, DNAR, palliative care pathway

**Theme:** Care planning
**Primary format:** Micro podcast
**Secondary format:** Text card

---

## Hook

Good end of life care is about making sure someone's final days or weeks reflect their wishes — that starts with good documentation and clear communication.

---

## Key points

- An advance care plan records a person's wishes for their care at the end of life — this should be in place well before a crisis
- A DNAR (Do Not Attempt Resuscitation) order is a medical decision made by a doctor — it must be documented and accessible but care staff do not make this decision
- A DNAR does not mean "do nothing" — continue to provide all other care, comfort, and dignity
- Palliative care focuses on comfort and quality of life rather than cure — pain management and emotional support are central
- Family and carers should be involved in end of life care planning with the service user's consent

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Service user is deteriorating and no advance care plan is in place?** → Raise with your manager urgently — there is still time to record wishes
- **Unsure whether a DNAR is in place for a service user?** → Check the care plan and ask your manager — never assume
- **Service user dies during your shift?** → Follow your organisation's procedure — do not move the person, contact your manager, and treat the situation with dignity and care

---

## Common mistakes

- Thinking that a DNAR means withdrawing all care — it only relates to CPR
- Not reading or following the advance care plan because it feels too difficult
- Not supporting colleagues after a death — end of life care is emotionally demanding

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — immediately when a service user dies or when end of life care begins
- **GP** — to confirm death and for clinical guidance
- **Family** — your manager will lead on notification
