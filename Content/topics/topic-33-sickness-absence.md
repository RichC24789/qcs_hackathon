# Topic 33: Sickness absence — reporting procedure, self-certification, SSP

**Theme:** HR & employment
**Primary format:** Text card
**Secondary format:** Scenario quiz

---

## Hook

Knowing how to report sickness correctly protects you — and makes it easier for your employer to support you properly.

---

## Key points

- Report sickness to your manager by phone before your shift starts — text and email are not usually sufficient unless your employer specifies otherwise
- Self-certification covers the first 7 days of sickness — after that a fit note from a GP is required
- Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) starts from the fourth day of consecutive absence — the first three days are waiting days and are not paid under SSP
- Your employer may have a company sick pay scheme that is more generous than SSP — check your contract
- A return to work meeting is standard practice after any period of sickness — this is supportive, not punitive

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Off sick for more than 7 days?** → Obtain a fit note from your GP and submit it to your manager
- **Concerned your sickness is being managed unfairly?** → Ask your manager to explain the policy, and consider raising a grievance if you feel the process is not being followed correctly
- **Returning to work after a long absence?** → A phased return or reasonable adjustments may be appropriate — discuss with your manager before your first day back

---

## Common mistakes

- Texting or messaging rather than calling — check your employer's specific reporting requirement
- Not obtaining a fit note after 7 days — this can affect SSP entitlement
- Not attending a return to work meeting — this is part of your employer's duty of care

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — before your shift on the first day of absence
- **HR** — for longer absences or if occupational health involvement is needed

---

## Quiz notes

Scenarios: off sick for 3 days — is SSP payable; off sick for 10 days with no fit note; returning after 6 weeks — what should be in place. Ask what the correct process is in each case.
