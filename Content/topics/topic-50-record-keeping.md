# Topic 50: Record keeping and documentation — what to record, accuracy, retention

**Theme:** Record keeping
**Primary format:** Micro podcast
**Secondary format:** Scenario quiz

---

## Hook

If it is not recorded, it did not happen — in care, records protect the service user, protect you, and tell the story of the care that was given.

---

## Key points

- Records must be accurate, factual, and written at the time of or immediately after care delivery — not reconstructed at the end of a shift
- Use objective language — what you observed, not what you assumed. "Service user appeared distressed and was crying" not "service user was upset about her family"
- Never alter a record after the fact without clearly noting that an amendment has been made, why, and by whom
- Records are confidential — only share with people who have a legitimate need to see them
- Data protection law gives service users the right to see their own records — requests must be handled promptly

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Made an error in a written record?** → Draw a single line through it, initial it, write the correct information alongside, and note the date of amendment — never use correction fluid
- **Unsure whether something is worth recording?** → If in doubt, record it — it is always easier to have information and not need it than to need it and not have it
- **Asked to share a service user's record with a third party?** → Check with your manager before sharing — confirm the person requesting has a legitimate right to see the information

---

## Common mistakes

- Writing records at the end of a shift from memory — details are forgotten and timing is inaccurate
- Using abbreviations that not everyone understands
- Deleting or overwriting digital records rather than amending them properly

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — for any request to share records, any record that has been altered improperly, or any suspected data breach
- **Your data protection officer** — for subject access requests or data breach concerns

---

## Podcast notes

Cover why records matter legally and clinically, how to write a good objective record, and what to do when a mistake is discovered. Include a scenario of a care worker asked to retrospectively change a record by a manager. Firm but supportive tone on the ethical dimension. 5 minutes.
