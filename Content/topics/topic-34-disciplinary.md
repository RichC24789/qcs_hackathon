# Topic 34: Disciplinary procedure — stages, gross misconduct, appeals

**Theme:** HR & employment
**Primary format:** Micro podcast
**Secondary format:** Text card

---

## Hook

Understanding the disciplinary process means you know what to expect and what your rights are — whether you are the subject of it or a manager running it.

---

## Key points

- The standard stages are: informal discussion → formal investigation → disciplinary hearing → outcome (no action, warning, final warning, or dismissal)
- You have the right to be accompanied to a disciplinary hearing by a colleague or trade union representative
- Gross misconduct — such as abuse, theft, or serious breach of confidentiality — may result in immediate suspension and dismissal without prior warnings
- You must be given written notice of a disciplinary hearing with enough time to prepare — usually at least 48 hours
- You have the right to appeal any disciplinary outcome within a specified timeframe

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Invited to a disciplinary hearing?** → You can bring a companion, prepare your account of events, and ask to see all evidence in advance
- **Suspended pending an investigation?** → Suspension is not a disciplinary sanction — it does not mean you are guilty of anything
- **Disagree with the outcome?** → Submit an appeal in writing within the timeframe specified in the outcome letter

---

## Common mistakes

- Assuming a disciplinary hearing is a formality — it has real consequences and should be taken seriously
- Not exercising the right to be accompanied
- Missing the appeal deadline

---

## Who to tell

- **HR or your line manager** — if you have questions about a disciplinary process
- **Trade union representative** — if you are a member
- **ACAS helpline (0300 123 1100)** — for free independent advice

---

## Podcast notes

Two-person format — one person has received a disciplinary hearing letter and is nervous, the other walks them through what to expect, their rights, and how to prepare. Reassuring and practical tone. 5 minutes.
