# Topic 25: RIDDOR reporting — what triggers a report, who submits it

**Theme:** Falls & incidents
**Primary format:** Text card
**Secondary format:** Scenario quiz

---

## Hook

RIDDOR reports are a legal requirement — not reporting when you should is a criminal offence, not just an admin oversight.

---

## Key points

- RIDDOR stands for Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences Regulations 2013
- Reportable events include: deaths, specified injuries (fractures, amputations, loss of consciousness), over-7-day incapacitation of a worker, occupational diseases, and dangerous occurrences
- Reports are made to the Health and Safety Executive (HSE) — your manager will usually do this
- Deaths and specified injuries must be reported immediately by phone; over-7-day injuries within 15 days
- RIDDOR applies to workers — incidents involving service users may be reported via other routes such as CQC

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Staff member has a fracture after a workplace incident?** → This is a specified injury — your manager must report to HSE immediately
- **Staff member is off work for more than 7 days following a workplace incident?** → Report to HSE within 15 days — the clock starts the day after the incident, not the first day of absence
- **Service user has a serious injury?** → RIDDOR may not apply but CQC notification likely does — speak to your manager

---

## Common mistakes

- Assuming RIDDOR only applies to serious accidents — a fracture, even a minor one, is a specified injury
- Not starting the clock on over-7-day absences from the day after the incident
- Thinking that because a service user was involved rather than a worker, RIDDOR does not apply — it may still apply if a worker was also injured

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — immediately for any workplace incident that may be reportable
- **HSE** — via riddor.hse.gov.uk or 0345 300 9923
- **Your insurance provider** — for serious incidents

---

## Quiz notes

Scenarios: staff member breaks wrist at work; service user falls and breaks hip; staff member is off sick for 10 days after a back injury at work. Ask which require a RIDDOR report.
