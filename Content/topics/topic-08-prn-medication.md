# Topic 8: PRN (as required) medication — protocols, reviewing, recording

**Theme:** Medication
**Primary format:** Text card
**Secondary format:** Scenario quiz

---

## Hook

PRN medication gives you flexibility — but only within a specific protocol. Without one, you should not be administering it.

---

## Key points

- PRN means "as required" — medication that is given when a specific symptom or condition occurs, not on a fixed schedule
- A PRN protocol must be in place for every PRN medication — it specifies what the medication is for, when to give it, the dose, and the maximum frequency
- Always check the MAR chart before giving a PRN medication — check when it was last given and whether the minimum interval has passed
- Record every PRN administration immediately, including the reason it was given
- PRN protocols should be reviewed regularly — at least every six months or when the service user's condition changes

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **No PRN protocol exists for a medication?** → Do not administer it — contact your manager and the GP to get a protocol in place first
- **Unsure whether to give PRN medication?** → Check the protocol — if the symptom matches and the interval has passed, proceed. If unsure, contact your manager
- **Service user is regularly needing PRN medication?** → This may indicate their regular medication needs reviewing — flag to your manager and GP

---

## Common mistakes

- Giving PRN medication without checking when it was last administered — this can lead to overdose
- Not recording the reason for giving PRN medication — the record should show what symptom prompted the dose
- Assuming a PRN protocol carries over automatically when a service user returns from hospital — always check it has been reauthorised

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — if no protocol exists, if you are unsure, or if a service user is regularly needing PRN medication
- **GP** — if the PRN medication appears to be needed more frequently than the protocol allows

---

## Quiz notes

Scenarios: PRN painkiller requested but given 2 hours ago and minimum interval is 4 hours; PRN medication with no protocol in place; service user asking for PRN medication for a symptom not covered by the protocol.
