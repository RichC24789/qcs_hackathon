# Topic 19: DoLS — when to apply, process

**Theme:** Safeguarding
**Primary format:** Text card
**Secondary format:** Scenario quiz

---

## Hook

If you are restricting someone's freedom to keep them safe, and they lack capacity to consent to that restriction, a DoLS authorisation is likely required.

---

## Key points

- DoLS stands for Deprivation of Liberty Safeguards — it is the legal process for authorising restrictions on a person who lacks capacity
- A deprivation of liberty occurs when someone is under continuous supervision and control and is not free to leave
- DoLS applications are made by the care home or hospital to the local authority
- An urgent authorisation can be self-granted for up to 7 days while a standard authorisation is sought
- DoLS applies in care homes and hospitals — a different process (Court of Protection) applies in other settings

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Service user lacks capacity and is being prevented from leaving for their own safety?** → Speak to your manager — a DoLS application may be needed
- **DoLS authorisation is in place — what does it mean day to day?** → The restrictions in the authorisation are legally permitted — do not exceed them and review the person's care plan regularly
- **Service user's condition has improved and they may now have capacity?** → Notify your manager — the DoLS may need to be reviewed or removed

---

## Common mistakes

- Not applying for DoLS because the restriction seems minor or is "for their own good" — the threshold is about the nature of the restriction, not the intention behind it
- Exceeding the restrictions permitted in the authorisation
- Not reviewing a DoLS regularly — they must be monitored throughout their duration

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — whenever a potential deprivation of liberty is identified
- **Local authority** — to apply for a standard authorisation
- **CQC** — DoLS records may be checked during inspection

---

## Quiz notes

Scenarios: service user with dementia tries to leave the building regularly and is redirected; service user is locked in their room at night for safety; service user says they want to go home. Ask whether each situation requires a DoLS application.
