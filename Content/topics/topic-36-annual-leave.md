# Topic 36: Annual leave — entitlement, booking notice, carry-over

**Theme:** HR & employment
**Primary format:** Text card
**Secondary format:** Quick reference card

---

## Hook

Your annual leave entitlement is a legal right — knowing how it works means you can plan confidently and avoid losing days you are owed.

---

## Key points

- The statutory minimum is 5.6 weeks per year (28 days for a full-time worker including bank holidays)
- Part-time workers receive the same entitlement on a pro-rata basis
- Your employer can set rules about when leave must be taken and how much notice you need to give — check your contract
- Bank holidays may or may not be included in your 28 days — check your contract
- Carrying leave over into the next holiday year is at your employer's discretion unless you could not take it due to sickness or maternity leave

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Request for leave refused?** → Your employer must give you a reason — if the refusal seems unreasonable, raise it with your manager or HR
- **Off sick during annual leave?** → You can reclaim those days as sick leave — notify your manager and provide a fit note if required
- **Leaving the organisation with unused leave?** → You are entitled to be paid for accrued but untaken leave

---

## Common mistakes

- Assuming all bank holidays are additional to the 28-day entitlement — they may be included
- Not checking the notice requirement before booking — late requests may be refused
- Losing leave at the end of the year by not planning ahead

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — to request and approve annual leave
- **HR** — for any disputes about entitlement or carry-over
