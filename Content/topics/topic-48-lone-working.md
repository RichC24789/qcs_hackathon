# Topic 48: Lone working — risk assessment, check-in procedures, safeguards

**Theme:** Health & safety
**Primary format:** Text card
**Secondary format:** Scenario quiz

---

## Hook

Lone working is part of many care roles — it does not have to be unsafe, but it does require planning and clear procedures.

---

## Key points

- A lone working risk assessment should be in place for any role that involves working without direct supervision
- Check-in procedures should be agreed with your manager — typically a call or message at the start and end of each visit
- Know what to do in an emergency — who to call, how to get help, and what information to give
- Trust your instincts — if a situation feels unsafe, leave and contact your manager
- Carry a charged phone at all times when lone working

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Service user or someone in the home becomes aggressive?** → Leave calmly if you can — your safety is the priority. Call your manager and 999 if needed once you are safe
- **Unable to gain access to a service user's home?** → Follow your organisation's missed visit procedure — contact your manager, try alternative contact numbers, and consider a welfare check if concerned
- **Something unexpected happens at a visit?** → Contact your manager before deciding how to proceed — do not make significant decisions alone

---

## Common mistakes

- Not checking in as agreed because the visit is busy — the check-in exists to confirm you are safe
- Staying in an unsafe situation because you feel responsible for completing the call
- Not reporting concerns about a visit location or service user's home environment

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — before, during, and after any visit where you have a concern
- **999** — if you are in immediate danger

---

## Quiz notes

Scenarios: no answer at door after 15 minutes; service user's relative becomes verbally aggressive; phone battery dies mid-shift. Ask what the correct response is.
