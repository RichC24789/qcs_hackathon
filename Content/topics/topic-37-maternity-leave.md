# Topic 37: Maternity leave — entitlement, pay, risk assessment, return to work

**Theme:** HR & employment
**Primary format:** Micro podcast
**Secondary format:** Quick reference card

---

## Hook

Maternity rights are more extensive than most people realise — knowing them early means you can plan properly and protect yourself.

---

## Key points

- You are entitled to up to 52 weeks of maternity leave regardless of how long you have worked for your employer
- Statutory Maternity Pay (SMP) is paid for 39 weeks — 90% of average weekly earnings for the first 6 weeks, then the flat statutory rate
- You must notify your employer of your pregnancy by the 15th week before your due date (around 25 weeks pregnant)
- Your employer must carry out a maternity risk assessment when you notify them of your pregnancy
- You have the right to return to the same job after ordinary maternity leave (the first 26 weeks)

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Pregnant and unsure when to tell your employer?** → You can tell them whenever you feel ready, but you must notify by 25 weeks to secure your rights — earlier notification triggers the risk assessment
- **Risk assessment has not been done?** → Request one from your manager — this is a legal obligation on your employer
- **Returning to work after maternity leave?** → You have the right to request flexible working — submit this at least 8 weeks before your return date

---

## Common mistakes

- Thinking you need to have worked for a certain period before you are entitled to maternity leave — you are entitled from day one
- Not understanding the difference between SMP (which has eligibility criteria) and maternity leave (which does not)
- Forgetting to request keeping in touch (KIT) days if you want to stay connected during leave

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — as soon as you feel ready, and formally by 25 weeks
- **HR** — to initiate the maternity leave process and confirm pay arrangements
