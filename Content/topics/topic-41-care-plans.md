# Topic 41: Care plan content and reviews — what to include, how often

**Theme:** Care planning
**Primary format:** Text card
**Secondary format:** Quick reference card

---

## Hook

A good care plan is a living document — not something written on admission and forgotten about.

---

## Key points

- A care plan must cover: personal details, assessed needs, goals, how care will be delivered, risks, and the person's preferences and wishes
- Care plans should be written with the service user, not just about them
- They must be reviewed at least every 6 months — and immediately following any significant change in health or circumstances
- Every staff member delivering care should read and follow the care plan — it is the instruction manual for that person's care
- Care plans must be stored securely and accessible to those who need them

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Care plan feels out of date?** → Raise with your manager — a review should be triggered
- **Service user's needs have changed significantly?** → Do not wait for the scheduled review — request an immediate update
- **Unsure how to carry out a task for a service user?** → Check the care plan first — if it does not cover the task, ask your manager before proceeding

---

## Common mistakes

- Writing care plans in professional jargon rather than plain language the service user can understand
- Treating the care plan as a static document that only changes at formal reviews
- Not involving the service user or their family in the review process

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — whenever a care plan appears out of date or does not reflect current needs
