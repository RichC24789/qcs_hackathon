# Topic 3: Medication administration — correct procedure, the six rights

**Theme:** Medication
**Primary format:** Poster
**Secondary format:** Animation

---

## Hook

Getting medication administration right every time starts with six simple checks — skip one and you risk serious harm.

---

## Key points

- The six rights are: right person, right medication, right dose, right route, right time, right documentation
- Always check the MAR chart before administering — never rely on memory
- Check the expiry date on every medication before giving it
- Never administer medication that is not in its original packaging or has no label
- Wash hands before and after administering medication
- Stay with the service user until you have confirmed they have taken the medication

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Not sure if medication has already been given?** → Check the MAR chart — if it shows as given, do not administer again. Contact your manager if unclear
- **Medication label does not match the MAR chart?** → Do not administer — contact your manager and the pharmacist before proceeding
- **Service user cannot swallow a tablet?** → Do not crush without explicit written authorisation from the prescriber — contact the GP

---

## Common mistakes

- Signing the MAR chart before giving the medication rather than immediately after
- Assuming a medication is the same because the box looks familiar — always read the label
- Leaving prepared medication unattended even briefly

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — if anything does not match or you are unsure about any step
- **GP or pharmacist** — if there is a discrepancy between the label and the MAR chart
- **Manager and GP** — if a service user cannot safely swallow their medication

---

## Poster notes

## Animation notes
