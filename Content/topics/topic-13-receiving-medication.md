# Topic 13: Medication received from pharmacy — booking in, stock checks

**Theme:** Medication
**Primary format:** Quick reference card
**Secondary format:** Animation

---

## Hook

Every time medication arrives from the pharmacy, it needs to be checked and recorded before it goes anywhere near a service user.

---

## Key points

- Check the delivery against the MAR chart — confirm the name, medication, dose, and quantity are correct
- Count the medication and record the quantity received in the stock log
- Check the expiry date on everything received
- Store the medication immediately in the correct location — room temperature or fridge as required
- If anything is missing, incorrect, or damaged, contact the pharmacy before accepting the delivery

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Medication received does not match the MAR chart?** → Do not accept it — contact the pharmacy immediately
- **Quantity received is less than expected?** → Record the discrepancy and contact the pharmacy — do not administer until resolved
- **Medication arrives damaged or with a broken seal?** → Do not accept it — return it to the pharmacy and document what happened

---

## Common mistakes

- Putting medication away without counting it or checking it against the MAR chart
- Not recording receipt in the stock log — if it is not recorded, you have no proof it arrived
- Accepting a delivery for the wrong service user without checking the label

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — if there is any discrepancy or problem with a delivery
- **Pharmacy** — for any errors or missing items in the delivery

---

## Quick reference card notes

Step-by-step booking-in checklist: check name and medication, count quantity, check expiry, record in stock log, store correctly. One page for the medication room.
