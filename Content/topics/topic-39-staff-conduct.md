# Topic 39: Staff conduct and professional boundaries — expectations, breaches

**Theme:** HR & employment
**Primary format:** Micro podcast
**Secondary format:** Scenario quiz

---

## Hook

Professional boundaries are not about being cold or distant — they are what make the care relationship safe for both you and the people you support.

---

## Key points

- Professional boundaries define the appropriate relationship between a care worker and a service user — based on the service user's needs, not personal preference
- Examples of boundary breaches include: accepting significant gifts, sharing personal contact details, forming a personal relationship outside work, and discussing personal problems with service users
- Social media and messaging apps carry particular risks — do not connect with service users or their families on personal accounts
- A boundary concern does not always mean bad intent — raise it and get support before a small issue becomes a bigger one
- Your employer's code of conduct sets out the specific expectations for your role

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Service user asks for your personal phone number?** → Decline politely — explain that you can only be contacted through the service
- **Offered a significant gift by a service user or their family?** → Decline and report to your manager — small tokens of appreciation may be acceptable but check your organisation's policy
- **Feeling uncomfortable about the nature of a relationship with a service user?** → Speak to your manager or supervisor — this is a normal thing to raise and shows professional awareness

---

## Common mistakes

- Assuming that because your intentions are good, a boundary breach does not matter
- Not reporting a boundary concern because you are worried about getting into trouble
- Allowing small boundary creep over time — each step may seem minor but the cumulative effect is significant

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager or supervisor** — for any boundary concern, yours or a colleague's
- **Safeguarding lead** — if a boundary breach may have resulted in harm

---

## Podcast notes

Use a scenario of a care worker who has been giving a service user their personal number "just in case" — explore how this started, why it felt kind at the time, and why it creates risk. Non-judgmental tone. 5 minutes.
