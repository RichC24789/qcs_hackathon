# Topic 20: Restraint and restrictive practices — policy, legislation, documentation

**Theme:** Safeguarding
**Primary format:** Micro podcast
**Secondary format:** Scenario quiz

---

## Hook

Any restriction on a person's movement or freedom — even a gentle hand on the arm — needs to be justified, proportionate, and documented.

---

## Key points

- Restrictive practices include physical restraint, chemical restraint (sedating medication), environmental restrictions (locked doors), and psychological restrictions (threats or consequences)
- Restraint must only be used as a last resort when someone is at risk of harm to themselves or others
- It must be proportionate — the minimum needed to manage the immediate risk
- Every use of restraint must be recorded, including what happened before, during, and after
- Positive Behaviour Support (PBS) approaches aim to reduce the need for restraint by addressing the root causes of behaviour

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Service user is becoming agitated and at risk of harming themselves?** → Use de-escalation techniques first — only move to physical intervention if there is immediate risk and no other option
- **Colleague uses restraint that seems excessive?** → This must be reported — excessive restraint is abuse
- **Restraint was used in an emergency?** → Record it immediately and report to your manager — a debrief should follow

---

## Common mistakes

- Using restraint as a first response rather than a last resort
- Not recording a restraint because it was brief or seemed minor
- Continuing restraint beyond the point at which the immediate risk has passed

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — after every use of restraint, immediately
- **Safeguarding lead** — if restraint appears excessive or inappropriate
- **CQC** — may need to be notified depending on the nature and frequency of restraint use

---

## Podcast notes

Cover the difference between planned restrictive practices (documented in a care plan) and emergency restraint. Use a scenario of a care worker unsure whether to physically intervene when a service user is trying to leave the building. Emphasise de-escalation first. 5 minutes.
