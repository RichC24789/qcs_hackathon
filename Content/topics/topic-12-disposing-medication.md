# Topic 12: Disposing of medication — procedure for unused or expired stock

**Theme:** Medication
**Primary format:** Animation
**Secondary format:** Poster

---

## Hook

Medication that is no longer needed must be disposed of correctly — you cannot just throw it in the bin or flush it away.

---

## Key points

- Unused or expired medication must be returned to a pharmacy for safe disposal — never put it in household waste
- Controlled drugs require a witnessed disposal process — two people must sign to confirm disposal
- Keep a record of all medication returned or disposed of, including the name, quantity, and date
- Do not dispose of medication on behalf of a service user without their knowledge and consent where possible
- If a service user dies, their medication must be stored securely and disposed of following your organisation's procedure

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Service user has unused medication after a course ends?** → Return it to the pharmacy with a record of the quantity returned
- **Controlled drug needs disposing of?** → Two staff members must be present — follow the controlled drugs disposal log process and contact your manager
- **Service user has died and has medication in their home?** → Do not remove anything until you have followed your organisation's policy — contact your manager immediately

---

## Common mistakes

- Flushing medication down the toilet — this causes environmental harm and is not an acceptable disposal method
- Disposing of a controlled drug without a witness or without completing the disposal record
- Not keeping a record of what was returned to the pharmacy

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — before disposing of any controlled drugs or medication belonging to a deceased service user
- **Pharmacist** — to arrange safe return and disposal
