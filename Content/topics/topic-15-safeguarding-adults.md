# Topic 15: Safeguarding adults — policy overview, principles, legislation

**Theme:** Safeguarding
**Primary format:** Micro podcast
**Secondary format:** Text card

---

## Hook

Safeguarding is everyone's responsibility — understanding the principles helps you act confidently when something doesn't feel right.

---

## Key points

- The Care Act 2014 sets out the legal framework for safeguarding adults in England
- The six safeguarding principles are: empowerment, prevention, proportionality, protection, partnership, and accountability
- An adult at risk is anyone aged 18 or over who has needs for care and support and is experiencing, or at risk of, abuse or neglect
- Safeguarding is not about removing risk entirely — it is about enabling people to live safely in a way that respects their choices
- Every organisation must have a designated safeguarding lead — know who yours is

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Unsure whether a situation requires a safeguarding referral?** → If in doubt, report — the safeguarding team will assess whether a formal enquiry is needed
- **Service user does not want action taken?** → Their wishes must be considered but do not override the duty to report — discuss with your manager
- **Concern involves a colleague?** → Follow the same process — report to your manager or the safeguarding lead

---

## Common mistakes

- Thinking safeguarding only applies to physical abuse — neglect, financial abuse, and emotional abuse are equally important
- Waiting for proof before reporting — a concern is sufficient
- Confusing safeguarding with health and safety — they are different, though sometimes overlapping

---

## Who to tell

- **Your safeguarding lead or manager** — first point of contact for any concern
- **Local authority safeguarding adults board** — for formal referrals or if internal routes are not working
- **CQC** — if the concern relates to the provider itself
