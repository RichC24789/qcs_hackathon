# Topic 9: Missed medication — what to do, reporting

**Theme:** Medication
**Primary format:** Animation
**Secondary format:** Poster

---

## Hook

A missed dose is not always a crisis — but how you respond and record it makes all the difference.

---

## Key points

- If you realise you have missed a dose, check the MAR chart and note the time the dose was due
- Contact your manager immediately — do not decide on your own whether to give the missed dose late
- Some medications can be given late, others cannot — always seek guidance from the GP or pharmacist
- Record the missed dose clearly on the MAR chart with the reason
- Monitor the service user for any signs of deterioration related to the missed medication

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Dose missed by less than an hour?** → Contact your manager — for some medications a late dose may be appropriate, but confirm first
- **Dose missed by several hours or a full round?** → Do not double up — contact your manager and the GP or pharmacist before doing anything
- **Service user was asleep or unavailable?** → Record the reason on the MAR chart, inform your manager, and follow up at the next opportunity

---

## Common mistakes

- Giving a double dose at the next administration to "catch up" — this is dangerous and never appropriate without GP guidance
- Not recording the missed dose because it feels like admitting a mistake — the record must reflect what actually happened
- Waiting until the end of the shift to tell your manager

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — immediately
- **GP or pharmacist** — if the medication is high risk or the dose was significantly delayed
- **111 or 999** — if the service user shows symptoms related to the missed medication

---

## Animation notes

Short sequence showing the correct steps: notice missed dose → check MAR chart → call manager → follow advice → record it. Emphasise not to double up. 60 seconds.
