# Topic 6: Medication competency assessments — frequency, sign-off process

**Theme:** Medication
**Primary format:** Text card
**Secondary format:** Quick reference card

---

## Hook

Medication competency isn't a one-off tick box — it needs to be reassessed regularly and whenever something changes.

---

## Key points

- All staff who administer medication must complete a competency assessment before doing so independently
- Competency should be reassessed at least annually — more frequently if errors have occurred
- A competency assessment typically includes a written or verbal knowledge check and a supervised practical observation
- Staff who fail a competency assessment should not administer medication until they have completed further training and been reassessed
- New medications or new routes of administration may require an additional competency sign-off

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Staff member's competency is overdue?** → They should not administer medication until reassessment is complete — arrange this as a priority
- **Staff member has made a medication error?** → A competency reassessment should be part of the response, alongside the incident investigation
- **New medication type introduced (e.g. a patch or injection)?** → Check whether a specific competency sign-off is required before the staff member administers it

---

## Common mistakes

- Treating competency as a one-time induction activity rather than an ongoing requirement
- Not triggering a reassessment after a medication error
- Allowing a competency to lapse because it is hard to schedule — lapsed competency is a serious governance risk

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — if a competency is overdue or a staff member has not passed
- **The staff member's supervisor** — to arrange training and reassessment
- **CQC** — may ask to see competency records during inspection

---

## Quick reference card notes

A simple table showing: competency type, who needs it, how often, and who signs it off. One page, designed for a staff file or noticeboard.
