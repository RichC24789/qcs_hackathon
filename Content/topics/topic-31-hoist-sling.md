# Topic 31: Hoist and sling use — safe use, checks, competency

**Theme:** Moving & handling
**Primary format:** Animation
**Secondary format:** Scenario quiz

---

## Hook

A hoist is only as safe as the person using it — never use one without training, and always check the equipment before you start.

---

## Key points

- Only staff who have been trained and assessed as competent should use a hoist
- Check the hoist before every use: battery level, condition of straps and hooks, sling condition, and LOLER inspection date
- The correct sling must be used for each service user — slings are individual and should not be shared
- Two staff should be present for hoisting in most circumstances
- Never leave a service user suspended in a hoist — always lower them promptly

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Sling appears worn or damaged?** → Do not use it — report to your manager and take it out of service
- **Hoist is not working or battery is flat?** → Do not attempt to use it — report to your manager
- **Service user becomes distressed during hoisting?** → Stop, lower them safely if possible, reassure them, and report to your manager

---

## Common mistakes

- Using a sling that belongs to another service user
- Not checking the LOLER inspection date — hoists must be inspected every 6 months
- Rushing the procedure and not communicating with the service user throughout

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — for any equipment fault, sling damage, or incident during hoisting
