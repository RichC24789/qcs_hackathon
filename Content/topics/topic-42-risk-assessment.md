# Topic 42: Risk assessment — types, templates, when to update

**Theme:** Care planning
**Primary format:** Text card
**Secondary format:** Quick reference card

---

## Hook

A risk assessment is not about preventing people from doing things — it is about making sure they can do them as safely as possible.

---

## Key points

- Common risk assessments in care include: falls, moving and handling, skin integrity, nutrition, environmental, lone working, and COSHH
- A risk assessment identifies hazards, assesses the likelihood and severity of harm, and puts controls in place to reduce risk
- Risk assessments must be reviewed regularly — at least annually and whenever circumstances change
- Positive risk taking — supporting someone to do something that carries risk because it matters to them — is valid and should be documented
- Risk assessments should be completed with the service user where possible

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **New service user admitted?** → A full set of risk assessments should be completed within the first 48–72 hours
- **Service user has had an incident?** → Review the relevant risk assessment immediately
- **Service user wants to do something that carries risk?** → Do not simply refuse — complete or review a risk assessment and explore how to support them safely

---

## Common mistakes

- Completing a risk assessment once and never reviewing it
- Using a risk assessment to justify refusing support rather than to find a way to provide it safely
- Not involving the service user in the process

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — whenever a new risk is identified or an existing assessment needs urgent review
