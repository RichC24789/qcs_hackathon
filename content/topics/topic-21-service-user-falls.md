# Topic 21: What to do when a service user falls — immediate steps, escalation

**Theme:** Falls & incidents
**Primary format:** Animation
**Secondary format:** Poster

---

## Hook

Falls are one of the most common incidents in care — knowing exactly what to do in the first few minutes can prevent further harm.

---

## Key points

- Do not move the person immediately — first check for signs of serious injury such as pain in the hip or leg, confusion, or loss of consciousness
- Stay calm and stay with the person — do not leave them alone on the floor
- Call 999 if you suspect a fracture, head injury, loss of consciousness, or if the person is on blood-thinning medication
- If it is safe to help them up, use the correct moving and handling technique — never drag or lift under the arms
- Complete a falls record and incident report before the end of your shift

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Person is unconscious, in severe pain, or unable to move a limb?** → Call 999 immediately, do not attempt to move them, stay with them until help arrives
- **Person is alert and says they are unharmed?** → Help them up safely using correct technique, monitor closely for 72 hours, and record the incident
- **Person hit their head?** → Even if they seem fine, inform your manager and monitor for signs of delayed concussion for at least 24 hours

---

## Common mistakes

- Moving the person too quickly — this can worsen an injury that is not yet visible
- Not recording the fall because the person seems unhurt — all falls must be documented
- Forgetting to notify the GP or family, especially when the person is on anticoagulants

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — as soon as the person is safe
- **999** — if there is any sign of serious injury
- **GP** — if the person is on blood-thinning medication or if you have any clinical concern
- **Family or next of kin** — your manager will advise
- **CQC** — only if the fall results in serious injury; your manager will lead on this

---

Step-by-step sequence: person on floor → do not move → assess for injury → call 999 if needed → call manager → help up safely if appropriate → record. 75 seconds.
