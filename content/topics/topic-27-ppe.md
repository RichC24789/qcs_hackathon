# Topic 27: PPE — correct use, donning and doffing, disposal

**Theme:** Infection control
**Primary format:** Animation
**Secondary format:** Poster

---

## Hook

PPE only works if you put it on and take it off in the right order — getting the doffing sequence wrong can contaminate you just as badly as wearing none at all.

---

## Key points

- Donning order: apron → gloves (hands should be clean before donning)
- Doffing order: gloves → apron → hand hygiene (this order prevents self-contamination)
- Face masks are required for aerosol-generating procedures or when caring for someone with a respiratory infection — follow current guidance
- Dispose of used PPE immediately in the correct waste stream — never leave used PPE on surfaces
- PPE is single-use — never wash or reuse disposable gloves or aprons

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Glove tears during a task?** → Remove it immediately, wash hands, and put on a fresh glove before continuing
- **Unsure which level of PPE is needed?** → Refer to the care plan and your organisation's PPE guidance — when in doubt use the higher level
- **PPE is uncomfortable or you find it hard to work in?** → Raise this with your manager — there may be alternative products available, but PPE cannot be omitted

---

## Common mistakes

- Touching the front of a used apron when removing it — roll it inward to contain contamination
- Snapping gloves off without controlling where they go
- Disposing of PPE in the general waste rather than the clinical waste stream

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — if PPE is unavailable, damaged, or if you have any concerns about PPE guidance
