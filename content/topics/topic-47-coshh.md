# Topic 47: COSHH — hazardous substances, safety data sheets, risk assessment

**Theme:** Health & safety
**Primary format:** Text card
**Secondary format:** Quick reference card

---

## Hook

Many everyday cleaning products are hazardous if used incorrectly — COSHH makes sure you know the risks before you open the bottle.

---

## Key points

- COSHH stands for Control of Substances Hazardous to Health — it covers chemicals, cleaning products, body fluids, and biological agents
- Every hazardous substance used in your workplace should have a Safety Data Sheet (SDS) — this tells you what the risks are and what PPE is needed
- Never mix cleaning products — some combinations produce toxic gases
- Store hazardous substances in their original containers in a locked cupboard away from service users
- Report any spillage of hazardous substances to your manager immediately

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Exposed to a hazardous substance (splash in eyes, skin contact, inhalation)?** → Follow the first aid guidance on the SDS — flush eyes with water for at least 10 minutes; seek medical attention if needed
- **Unsure what PPE to use for a cleaning task?** → Check the SDS — it will specify the required PPE
- **Hazardous substance stored incorrectly?** → Report to your manager — it must be secured away from service users

---

## Common mistakes

- Decanting cleaning products into unmarked containers
- Not checking the SDS before using a new product
- Mixing bleach-based and ammonia-based products — this produces toxic chloramine gas

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — for any COSHH incident, spillage, or storage concern
