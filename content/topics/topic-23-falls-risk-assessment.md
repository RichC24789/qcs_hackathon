# Topic 23: Falls risk assessment — tools, frequency, post-fall monitoring

**Theme:** Falls & incidents
**Primary format:** Text card
**Secondary format:** Quick reference card

---

## Hook

A falls risk assessment does not prevent all falls — but it gives you the information to reduce risk and respond effectively when one happens.

---

## Key points

- Every service user should have a falls risk assessment completed on admission and reviewed at least every six months or after any fall
- Common risk factors include: cognitive impairment, previous falls, mobility problems, certain medications, poor vision, and environmental hazards
- The assessment should lead to a care plan that addresses identified risks — it is not just a form to complete
- After a fall, a post-fall assessment should be completed and the care plan updated
- Environmental checks are part of falls prevention — look for trip hazards, poor lighting, and unsuitable footwear

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Service user has just had their first fall?** → Review the falls risk assessment immediately and update the care plan
- **Service user is falling repeatedly?** → Escalate to the GP — there may be a medical cause such as a change in medication or a new health condition
- **Environmental hazard identified?** → Remove it or report it immediately — document what action was taken

---

## Common mistakes

- Completing the risk assessment as a form-filling exercise without linking it to the care plan
- Not reviewing the assessment after a fall — the fall itself is important new information
- Focusing only on the service user and not on environmental factors

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — after every fall and when risks are identified
- **GP** — if falls are increasing in frequency or have a possible medical cause
- **Family** — should be informed of falls, especially repeated ones
