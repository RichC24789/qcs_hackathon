# Topic 43: Consent to care — how to obtain, when needed, documentation

**Theme:** Care planning
**Primary format:** Micro podcast
**Secondary format:** Scenario quiz

---

## Hook

Consent is not a signature on a form — it is an ongoing conversation that must happen before every care interaction.

---

## Key points

- Consent must be freely given, informed, and specific — a blanket consent signed on admission does not cover every future interaction
- Before every care task, explain what you are going to do and wait for agreement — verbal consent is valid
- People have the right to refuse care even if refusing carries risk — respect this and document it
- If someone lacks capacity to consent, a best interest decision must be in place before care is given
- Document consent and any refusals clearly in the care record

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Service user refuses personal care?** → Do not proceed — note the refusal, record it, and inform your manager. Try again later and explore whether there is a reason for the refusal
- **Service user has dementia and cannot give verbal consent?** → Look for non-verbal signs of agreement or distress — and ensure a best interest decision is in place for care tasks
- **Family member says it is fine to proceed even though the service user is saying no?** → The service user's wishes take precedence unless a valid best interest decision has been made

---

## Common mistakes

- Proceeding with care because the service user "always agrees" without checking each time
- Treating consent as a one-time administrative step rather than an ongoing conversation
- Not documenting refusals because they feel like a failure

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — whenever consent is refused or there is uncertainty about capacity

---

## Podcast notes

Explore the difference between consent on admission and consent in the moment. Use a scenario where a service user with dementia repeatedly refuses a shower. Cover best interest, non-verbal consent, and documenting refusals. 5 minutes.
