# Topic 26: Infection control procedures — standard precautions, hand hygiene

**Theme:** Infection control
**Primary format:** Animation
**Secondary format:** Poster

---

## Hook

Standard precautions apply with every service user, every time — not just when someone is known to have an infection.

---

## Key points

- Standard precautions include: hand hygiene, PPE, safe disposal of sharps and waste, and clean working practices
- Wash hands for at least 20 seconds using the correct technique — before and after every contact with a service user
- Alcohol gel is effective for most situations but does not replace soap and water when hands are visibly dirty or after contact with Norovirus or C. difficile
- Change gloves and apron between service users — never reuse PPE
- Any suspected infection must be reported to your manager and may require isolation procedures

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Service user shows signs of infection (fever, diarrhoea, vomiting, wound discharge)?** → Report to your manager, follow isolation guidance, and increase PPE use
- **Running low on PPE?** → Report to your manager immediately — never work without appropriate PPE
- **Unsure whether to use gloves for a task?** → Apply standard precautions — if there is any risk of contact with bodily fluids, use gloves

---

## Common mistakes

- Using alcohol gel instead of soap and water when hands are visibly contaminated
- Touching your face or phone after glove removal before washing hands
- Wearing the same apron across multiple rooms or service users

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — whenever infection is suspected or PPE is running low
- **GP or district nurse** — for clinical advice about a service user's infection
- **Local health protection team** — your manager will contact for outbreaks
