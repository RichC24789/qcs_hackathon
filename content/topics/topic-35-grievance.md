# Topic 35: Grievance procedure — how to raise, timescales, outcomes

**Theme:** HR & employment
**Primary format:** Micro podcast
**Secondary format:** Text card

---

## Hook

A grievance is a formal way of raising a concern about your treatment at work — knowing how to use it means you do not have to suffer in silence.

---

## Key points

- A grievance is a complaint about your employer, a colleague, or a working condition
- Raise it informally first where appropriate — a conversation can resolve many issues without a formal process
- If the issue is not resolved informally, submit a written grievance to your manager or HR
- Your employer must acknowledge the grievance promptly and hold a hearing within a reasonable timeframe
- You have the right to be accompanied at a grievance hearing and to appeal the outcome

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Concern is about your manager?** → Submit the grievance to HR or your manager's manager
- **Grievance concerns bullying or harassment?** → This follows the same formal process — document specific incidents with dates and details
- **Grievance outcome is unsatisfactory?** → Appeal in writing within the timeframe specified

---

## Common mistakes

- Not putting the grievance in writing — verbal complaints are harder to track and follow up
- Waiting too long to raise a concern — issues are easier to address when they are current
- Not keeping a personal record of incidents, conversations, and correspondence

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager or HR** — to submit a grievance
- **Trade union representative** — if you are a member
- **ACAS (0300 123 1100)** — for free advice

---

## Podcast notes

Walk through a scenario of a care worker who feels they are being treated unfairly by their shift leader. Cover the informal and formal routes, what to document, and how to prepare for a hearing. 5 minutes.
