# Topic 49: Food safety and nutrition — hydration, dietary needs, kitchen hygiene

**Theme:** Health & safety
**Primary format:** Poster
**Secondary format:** Text card

---

## Hook

Poor nutrition and dehydration are among the most common but preventable causes of deterioration in care — food and drink are part of clinical care, not just hospitality.

---

## Key points

- An adult needs approximately 1.5–2 litres of fluid per day — more in hot weather or if unwell
- Signs of dehydration include: dark urine, dry mouth, confusion, dizziness, and reduced urine output
- Dietary needs must be recorded in the care plan — allergies, religious requirements, texture modifications, and preferences
- Food must be stored, prepared, and served at safe temperatures — hot food above 63°C, cold food below 5°C
- Kitchen hygiene basics: wash hands before handling food, separate raw and cooked food, clean surfaces before and after use

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Service user seems confused and has not been drinking?** → Offer fluids, monitor, record intake, and report to your manager — this may indicate dehydration
- **Service user requires a modified texture diet and you are unsure of the correct preparation?** → Check the care plan and speak to your manager before serving — incorrect texture food is a choking risk
- **Food temperature is not within safe range?** → Do not serve it — reheat or discard following food safety guidelines

---

## Common mistakes

- Assuming a service user is drinking enough because they have not complained of thirst — thirst sensation reduces with age
- Not checking the care plan for dietary requirements before preparing food
- Serving food without checking the temperature

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — for any concerns about a service user's nutrition or hydration
- **GP or dietitian** — for significant weight loss or nutritional concerns
