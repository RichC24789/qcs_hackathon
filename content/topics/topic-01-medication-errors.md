# Topic 1: Medication errors — what to do, reporting, root cause

**Theme:** Medication
**Primary format:** Animation
**Secondary format:** Scenario quiz

---

## Hook

Making a medication mistake is stressful, but what you do in the next few minutes matters more than the mistake itself.

---

## Key points

- Do not try to correct the error quietly or hope it resolves itself — always report immediately
- Check on the service user straight away and look for any signs of adverse reaction
- Record exactly what happened, what was given, and at what time — be factual, not defensive
- Contact your manager and, if clinically urgent, call 111 or 999 without waiting for permission
- Complete an incident report before the end of your shift while details are fresh

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Wrong medication given, or wrong dose?** → Call your manager immediately, monitor the service user, and call 999 if they show any adverse symptoms
- **Correct medication given but recorded incorrectly?** → Inform your manager, correct the record with a note explaining the amendment, and document why the error occurred
- **Medication given at the wrong time?** → Inform your manager and check with the GP or pharmacist whether any action is needed before the next dose

---

## Common mistakes

- Waiting to see if the service user is affected before reporting — always report first, monitor second
- Crossing out or deleting the incorrect entry rather than drawing a single line through it, initialling it, and writing the correct information alongside
- Assuming a near-miss doesn't need reporting — if the error was caught before administration it still needs to be recorded so patterns can be identified

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — immediately
- **GP or pharmacist** — if clinical advice is needed about the medication given
- **111 or 999** — if the service user shows any sign of adverse reaction
- **CQC** — if the error results in serious harm (your manager will lead on this)
- **Family or next of kin** — your manager will advise on timing and what to say

---

## Animation notes

Show a step-by-step sequence: discover error → stay with service user → call manager → complete incident report. Keep to 60–90 seconds. No voiceover needed if captions are clear.

## Quiz notes

Scenario-based: present 2–3 situations (wrong drug given, recording error, near miss) and ask what the correct first action is. Include explanation with each answer.
