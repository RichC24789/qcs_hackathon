# Topic 7: Covert medication — policy, best interest, documentation

**Theme:** Medication
**Primary format:** Micro podcast
**Secondary format:** Text card

---

## Hook

Giving medication without a person's knowledge is only ever legal in very specific circumstances — and every step must be documented and agreed in advance.

---

## Key points

- Covert medication means giving medication without the service user's knowledge, usually in food or drink
- It is only lawful if the service user lacks mental capacity and a best interest decision has been made and documented
- A covert medication plan must be in place before any covert administration — this must be agreed by the GP, pharmacist, and relevant family or advocates
- The plan must be reviewed regularly and whenever the service user's condition changes
- Every covert administration must be recorded on the MAR chart

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **No covert medication plan exists but someone suggests hiding medication in food?** → Do not do it — this could constitute abuse. Speak to your manager immediately
- **Service user has a covert medication plan in place?** → Follow it exactly as documented — do not deviate without a review
- **Service user's capacity changes and they can now consent?** → The covert medication plan must be reviewed and may need to be stopped — contact the GP and your manager

---

## Common mistakes

- Assuming it is fine to hide medication because the service user "always refuses" — this is never acceptable without a formal plan
- Not involving the pharmacist — some medications cannot safely be crushed or mixed with food
- Failing to document each covert administration on the MAR chart

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — before any covert administration begins
- **GP** — must be involved in approving the covert medication plan
- **Pharmacist** — must confirm the medication can safely be given covertly
- **Family or advocate** — should be involved in the best interest decision
- **CQC** — may ask to see covert medication plans during inspection

---

## Podcast notes

Two-person format. Open with a scenario: a carer is asked by a colleague to put medication in a service user's porridge because "they always spit it out." Walk through why this is not acceptable, what the correct process is, and what documentation needs to be in place. 4–5 minutes.
