# Topic 40: Safer recruitment — DBS checks, references, right to work

**Theme:** HR & employment
**Primary format:** Quick reference card
**Secondary format:** Text card

---

## Hook

Safer recruitment is not just a legal requirement — it is the first line of defence in protecting vulnerable people from harm.

---

## Key points

- An enhanced DBS check is required for all staff working with vulnerable adults or children — this must be in place before unsupervised work begins
- Two references should be obtained before employment starts — at least one from the most recent employer
- Right to work checks must be completed for every new employee — this includes checking original documents
- Gaps in employment history should be explored and documented at interview
- DBS checks should be renewed every three years, or more frequently if your organisation's policy requires it

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **DBS check has not come back before a new starter's first day?** → They should be supervised at all times until the check is received and clear
- **DBS check reveals a conviction?** → Follow your organisation's policy — not all convictions are automatically disqualifying; a risk assessment is required
- **Reference raises a concern about a candidate?** → Do not proceed to employment without discussing with HR or your manager

---

## Common mistakes

- Allowing unsupervised work before a DBS check is received
- Accepting a copy of a DBS certificate from a previous employer rather than applying for a new one
- Not checking the DBS update service to see if a certificate is still current

---

## Who to tell

- **HR or your manager** — for any concerns arising from recruitment checks
- **DBS (03000 200 190)** — for queries about the checking process

---

Recruitment checks checklist: DBS, references (number and type), right to work documents, employment history gaps. One page for managers and HR.
