# Topic 46: Fire safety — evacuation procedure, fire checks, drills

**Theme:** Health & safety
**Primary format:** Animation
**Secondary format:** Poster

---

## Hook

In a fire, every second counts — knowing the evacuation procedure before an emergency means you act rather than freeze.

---

## Key points

- Know the location of all fire exits, fire extinguishers, and the fire alarm call points in your building
- If you discover a fire: raise the alarm, call 999, begin evacuation — do not attempt to fight the fire unless trained and safe to do so
- Every service user should have a Personal Emergency Evacuation Plan (PEEP) — know the plans for the people you are caring for
- Fire doors must be kept closed — never prop them open
- Fire drills must be carried out regularly — take them seriously as practice for a real event

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Fire alarm sounds?** → Do not investigate — begin evacuation immediately following the PEEP for each service user
- **Fire in a room you cannot safely pass?** → Do not enter — alert others, close doors to contain the fire, and wait for the fire service
- **Service user refuses to evacuate?** → Follow their PEEP — if it is safe, use persuasion; if immediate danger, a proportionate physical assist may be necessary

---

## Common mistakes

- Propping fire doors open for convenience — this allows fire and smoke to spread
- Not knowing the PEEP for service users in your care before an emergency occurs
- Treating fire drills as an inconvenience rather than essential practice

---

## Who to tell

- **999** — immediately upon discovering a fire
- **Your manager** — after any fire alarm activation or drill debrief

---

## Animation notes

Clear step-by-step evacuation sequence: hear alarm → do not investigate → alert others → begin evacuation following PEEP → assemble at muster point → call 999. 60 seconds.

## Poster notes

Evacuation steps numbered clearly. Fire exit symbol prominent. Space to add building-specific muster point. A4 landscape for corridor display.
