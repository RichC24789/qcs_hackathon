# Topic 14: Recognising and reporting abuse — signs, types, who to tell

**Theme:** Safeguarding
**Primary format:** Micro podcast
**Secondary format:** Poster

---

## Hook

You do not need to be certain that abuse is happening to report it — a concern is enough, and reporting it is always the right thing to do.

---

## Key points

- The main types of abuse are: physical, emotional, sexual, financial, neglect, discriminatory, institutional, and modern slavery
- Signs of abuse can be physical (unexplained injuries, poor hygiene) or behavioural (withdrawal, anxiety, fear around a particular person)
- You have a duty to report any concern — it is not your job to investigate, just to report
- Report to your manager first, and to the local authority safeguarding team if your manager is the subject of the concern
- Never promise a service user confidentiality if they disclose abuse — explain that you may need to share what they tell you to keep them safe

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Service user discloses abuse to you?** → Listen, do not promise confidentiality, do not ask leading questions, write down what was said in their own words, and report to your manager immediately
- **You observe something that concerns you but are not sure it is abuse?** → Report it anyway — it is not your job to decide. Record what you saw factually
- **Your manager is the subject of the concern?** → Report directly to the local authority safeguarding adults team

---

## Common mistakes

- Not reporting because you are not 100% certain — concern is enough to trigger a report
- Promising a service user that you will keep what they tell you secret
- Investigating yourself rather than reporting and letting the safeguarding process take over

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — immediately for any safeguarding concern
- **Local authority safeguarding adults team** — if your manager is implicated or if the concern is not acted on
- **Police** — if a crime may have been committed (your manager will usually lead on this)
- **CQC** — your manager will notify if required

---

## Podcast notes

Two-person format. Open with a scenario — a carer notices unexplained bruising on a service user who becomes anxious when a particular colleague is mentioned. Walk through recognising the signs, what to say to the service user, and the reporting process. 4–5 minutes.

## Poster notes

Types of abuse with 2–3 key signs for each. Bold, clear layout. Include escalation contacts. A4 portrait for staff room.
