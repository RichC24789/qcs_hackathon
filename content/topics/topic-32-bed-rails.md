# Topic 32: Bed rails — risk assessment, consent, checking frequency

**Theme:** Moving & handling
**Primary format:** Text card
**Secondary format:** Quick reference card

---

## Hook

Bed rails can increase safety for some people but increase risk for others — the decision to use them must always be based on an individual assessment, never routine practice.

---

## Key points

- A bed rail risk assessment must be completed before rails are used — this should be reviewed regularly and after any incident
- Bed rails must never be used without the informed consent of the service user or, where they lack capacity, a best interest decision
- Check bed rail condition regularly — look for gaps between the mattress and rail that could cause entrapment
- Rails should not be used simply to prevent a service user from getting up — this may constitute a deprivation of liberty
- Document the decision to use or not use bed rails clearly in the care plan

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Service user requests bed rails?** → Complete a risk assessment, obtain consent, document the decision, and check regularly
- **Concerned that bed rails are being used inappropriately?** → Raise with your manager — if someone is being prevented from getting up without a best interest decision this may require safeguarding or DoLS consideration
- **Gap identified between mattress and bed rail?** → Take the rails out of use immediately and report to your manager — entrapment can be fatal

---

## Common mistakes

- Using bed rails as a default for every service user at risk of falling
- Not reviewing the risk assessment when a service user's condition changes
- Failing to check for gaps during daily checks

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — for any concerns about bed rail use or condition
- **Occupational therapist** — for complex assessments
