# Topic 5: Medication storage — temperatures, fridge checks, room conditions

**Theme:** Medication
**Primary format:** Poster
**Secondary format:** Quick reference card

---

## Hook

Storing medication incorrectly can make it ineffective or dangerous — and it's one of the easiest things to get right.

---

## Key points

- Room temperature medications should be stored between 15°C and 25°C — check your medication room thermometer daily
- Fridge medications must be kept between 2°C and 8°C — check and record the fridge temperature every day
- If the fridge temperature goes outside range, contact your manager and the pharmacist before administering any fridge medication
- Medications must be stored in their original packaging with the original label intact
- Never store medication in a bathroom or near a heat source — humidity and heat degrade medication

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Fridge temperature is outside 2–8°C?** → Do not administer fridge medication — record the reading, contact your manager and pharmacist immediately
- **Medication room is too warm?** → Record it, move medications if safe to do so, contact your manager
- **Medication has been left out of the fridge?** → Do not assume it is still safe — contact the pharmacist before administering

---

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting to record temperature checks — if it is not recorded it did not happen
- Putting insulin or eye drops in the door of the fridge where temperature fluctuates most
- Not checking the room thermometer because the room feels fine

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — if any temperature is out of range
- **Pharmacist** — before administering any medication that may have been compromised
- **GP** — if a service user may have received degraded medication

---

## Poster notes

Two clear panels — one for room temperature (15–25°C), one for fridge (2–8°C). Bold temperature ranges, simple visuals. Include a daily checklist strip at the bottom. A4 portrait for the medication room wall.
