# Topic 38: Probationary period — review process, extension, ending employment

**Theme:** HR & employment
**Primary format:** Text card
**Secondary format:** Scenario quiz

---

## Hook

Probation is a two-way process — it gives your employer time to assess your performance and gives you time to assess whether the role is right for you.

---

## Key points

- A typical probationary period in care is 3–6 months — check your contract for the specific length
- During probation you should receive regular supervision and feedback — at least once a month
- At the end of probation a formal review should be held — you should be told whether you have passed, whether it is being extended, or whether employment is ending
- Probation can be extended if performance or conduct concerns have been identified — you should be told clearly what needs to improve and given support to do so
- Employment can be ended during probation with shorter notice — the statutory minimum is one week if you have been employed for one month or more

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Not received any supervision or feedback during probation?** → Raise this with your manager — you are entitled to support and feedback
- **Probation is being extended?** → Ask for the concerns in writing and what specific improvements are expected
- **Probation review has not been held at the end of the period?** → Chase your manager — a missed review does not automatically mean you have passed

---

## Common mistakes

- Assuming that if nothing is said during probation it means everything is fine
- Not raising your own concerns or questions during probation supervision
- Assuming dismissal during probation is unfair — notice periods are shorter and the process is less formal

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — for any concerns during probation
- **HR** — if probation is ending employment and you have questions about process or pay

---

## Quiz notes

Scenarios: 4 months into a 3-month probation with no review held; told probation is being extended with no written reasons; asked to sign probation confirmation form without seeing feedback. Ask what the correct response is.
