# Topic 28: Outbreak management — isolation, cleaning, notifications

**Theme:** Infection control
**Primary format:** Text card
**Secondary format:** Quick reference card

---

## Hook

An outbreak spreads fast in a care setting — the first few hours of response make the biggest difference.

---

## Key points

- An outbreak is usually defined as two or more cases of the same infection within a short period in the same setting
- Isolate affected service users to their rooms as quickly as possible — with their agreement where possible
- Increase cleaning frequency — particularly high-touch surfaces like door handles, call bells, and bathroom fittings
- Staff who are symptomatic should not come to work — report to your manager immediately
- Notify the local health protection team through your manager — they will advise on further action

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **One service user has diarrhoea and vomiting?** → Isolate them, increase cleaning, monitor other service users closely, and report to your manager
- **Two or more service users have the same symptoms?** → Declare an outbreak — your manager should notify the health protection team immediately
- **Staff member develops symptoms during a shift?** → They should go home immediately, follow the exclusion period guidance, and not return until symptom-free for the required time

---

## Common mistakes

- Waiting to see if more cases develop before declaring an outbreak — early action limits spread
- Not isolating until a test result confirms the infection
- Allowing symptomatic staff to stay on shift because of staffing pressures

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — immediately for any suspected outbreak
- **Local health protection team** — your manager will notify
- **CQC** — outbreaks may need to be notified depending on severity
- **Family members** — should be informed that an outbreak is occurring

---

## Quick reference card notes

Outbreak response checklist: isolation steps, cleaning schedule, staff exclusion rules, notification contacts. One page for the manager's office.
