# Topic 44: Mental health and wellbeing of service users — policy, escalation

**Theme:** Care planning
**Primary format:** Micro podcast
**Secondary format:** Text card

---

## Hook

Mental health is as important as physical health — noticing a change in mood or behaviour and knowing what to do can make an enormous difference.

---

## Key points

- Changes in behaviour, mood, sleep, appetite, or social withdrawal can all be signs of deteriorating mental health
- Record observations factually and report changes to your manager — do not wait for a scheduled review
- Service users have the right to have their mental health needs addressed in their care plan
- Crisis situations — where a service user may be at risk of harming themselves or others — require immediate escalation
- Your own mental health matters too — speak to your manager if you are finding care work emotionally difficult

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Service user seems significantly lower in mood than usual?** → Document your observations and report to your manager — a GP or mental health review may be needed
- **Service user mentions thoughts of self-harm or suicide?** → Take it seriously — do not leave them alone, contact your manager immediately, and call 999 if you believe they are in immediate danger
- **Service user is becoming increasingly agitated or presenting unusual behaviour?** → Document, report to your manager, and follow de-escalation guidance in the care plan

---

## Common mistakes

- Attributing behavioural changes to "just having a bad day" without documenting and reporting
- Not knowing whether a service user has a mental health diagnosis or care plan in place
- Feeling that mental health concerns are outside your scope — observing and reporting is always within your role

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — for any concerns about a service user's mental health
- **GP or community mental health team** — your manager will refer if needed
- **999** — if there is immediate risk of harm

---

## Podcast notes

Cover how to recognise deterioration in mental health, how to talk to someone who is struggling, and how to look after yourself when caring for people with complex mental health needs. Warm and human tone. 5 minutes.
