# Topic 4: Controlled drugs — storage, counting, handover, documentation

**Theme:** Medication
**Primary format:** Micro podcast
**Secondary format:** Quick reference card

---

## Hook

Controlled drugs have stricter rules than other medications — and the consequences of getting those rules wrong are serious for you and the service user.

---

## Key points

- Controlled drugs must be stored in a locked, fixed cabinet — separate from other medications
- Two people must check and sign for controlled drugs at every administration
- A running stock count must be maintained and checked at every handover
- Any discrepancy in the count must be reported to your manager immediately — do not wait
- Controlled drugs cannot be disposed of by care staff alone — a witnessed disposal process must be followed

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Stock count does not add up at handover?** → Do not proceed — report to your manager immediately and do not administer until the discrepancy is resolved
- **Controlled drug has not been given but is signed for?** → This is a serious recording error — report to your manager immediately
- **Service user no longer needs a controlled drug?** → Contact the GP and pharmacist — do not dispose of it yourself

---

## Common mistakes

- Only one person signing for a controlled drug administration — two signatures are always required
- Leaving the controlled drugs cabinet unlocked even briefly
- Not recording a refused dose in the controlled drugs register

---

## Who to tell

- **Your manager** — immediately for any discrepancy, error, or concern
- **GP** — if a controlled drug needs to be stopped or reviewed
- **Pharmacist** — for disposal or return of unused controlled drugs
- **Police** — if theft is suspected (your manager will lead on this)
- **CQC** — for serious incidents involving controlled drugs

---

## Podcast notes

Two-person format. One person plays a new staff member asking questions, the other a senior carer explaining the rules. Open with a real scenario — a stock discrepancy discovered at handover — and work through what to do. 4–5 minutes.

## Quick reference card notes

One-page checklist: storage requirements, two-person check process, handover count steps, what to do if something doesn't add up. Designed for the medication room wall.
