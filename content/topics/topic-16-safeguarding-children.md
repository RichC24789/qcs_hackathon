# Topic 16: Safeguarding children — reporting, thresholds, local contacts

**Theme:** Safeguarding
**Primary format:** Micro podcast
**Secondary format:** Quick reference card

---

## Hook

If you work with or around children, even occasionally, you need to know what to do if you have a concern — the threshold for reporting is lower than most people think.

---

## Key points

- Children's safeguarding is governed by the Children Act 1989 and Working Together to Safeguard Children 2023
- A child is anyone under 18 years old
- You do not need evidence of abuse to make a referral — reasonable concern is enough
- Report to your designated safeguarding lead, who will contact children's social care if needed
- If a child is in immediate danger, call 999 first

---

## Quick decision prompt

- **Child discloses abuse or neglect to you?** → Listen without asking leading questions, reassure them, do not promise confidentiality, and report to your safeguarding lead immediately
- **You are concerned about a service user's child but have not seen direct evidence?** → Report your concern — it is not your role to investigate
- **Child is in immediate danger?** → Call 999 first, then your manager

---

## Common mistakes

- Assuming children's safeguarding is only relevant in children's services — it applies anywhere a child could be present
- Not knowing who the designated safeguarding lead is in your organisation
- Waiting to see if the situation resolves before reporting

---

## Who to tell

- **Your designated safeguarding lead** — immediately for any concern
- **Children's social care** — your safeguarding lead will make this referral
- **999** — if a child is in immediate danger
- **NSPCC helpline (0808 800 5000)** — if internal routes are not available
